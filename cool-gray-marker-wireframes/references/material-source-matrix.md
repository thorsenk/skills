# Marker-material research sources

This matrix records the real-material evidence used for the materials-only calibration candidate. The linked images and video frames are research references, not package assets. Their copyright remains with their creators. Do not copy them into an installed or released skill without permission.

## Primary matched specimen family

| Source | Evidence role | What it establishes | Limits |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| [Copic Official — Drawing Lines](https://copic.jp/en/howto/howto-nib/) and its [official video](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fKY3BH7UWQQ) | Primary real-material sequence | Medium Broad nib width and direction; translucent one-pass coverage; longitudinal variation; darker start and stop deposits; one-pass versus repeated-pass buildup; directional filled regions; darker overlaps | Demonstration colors are green, blue, and yellow rather than cool gray. Material behavior is evaluated before color. The sequence does not contain graphite. |
| [Copic Official — Gradation & Texture](https://copic.jp/en/howto/marker/) and its [official video](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NRcm2q-42r4) | Primary real-material sequence for turns and blends | Circular wet application, curved/scalloped edges, visible pass merging, and wet-on-wet blending | Uses the Super Brush nib for the curved specimen. It supports edge and blending behavior but does not replace the Medium Broad nib-width reference. |

Frames selected from either official sequence must retain their original image content. Cropping and scaling are allowed; recoloring, texture overlays, noise, blur, displacement, and synthetic edge treatment are not.

## Supporting material boundaries

| Source | Evidence role | What it establishes | Limits |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| [Copic Official — Paper Selections](https://copic.jp/en/product/paper/) | Paper and bleeding boundary | Copic dye ink soaks into paper; paper texture and absorbency change edge spread, finish, and blending. The photographed five-paper comparison makes controlled versus excessive feathering visible. | The comparison uses blue ink and different papers, so it is a boundary study rather than part of the matched stroke sequence. |
| [Marker Novice — C3 Cool Gray No. 3](https://markernovice.com/uncapped/c3) | Cool-gray color and layer anchor | A documented C3 swatch on X-Press It Blending Card with one through four layers | Supports cool-gray value buildup only. It does not establish nib motion, graphite behavior, or release-ready color values. |
| [Copic Official — Intro to Copic Classic 2](https://copicmarkers.com/blogs/educational/intro-classic-2) | Broad-nib and mixed-media context | The broad nib's flat side creates broad strokes; repeated broad-nib passes blend and deepen; finished examples combine marker with pencil or graphite-like linework | Finished illustrations are application examples, not controlled close-up tests. |
| [Copic PM Paper](https://copicmarkers.com/products/copic-pencil-marker-pm-paper) | Marker-and-pencil compatibility | PM Paper is designed for marker rendering with pencil or pastel accents and resists bleed-through | Product documentation, not a matched graphite swatch. |
| [Marianne Walker — Pencils and Marker, Part 1: Graphite](https://ilikemarkers.blogspot.com/2008/08/pencils-and-marker-part-1-graphite.html) | Graphite interaction boundary | Alcohol-marker solvent can move graphite and then seal it; light graphite is safer beneath marker; final graphite detail is better placed after dry marker | The close-up demonstrates marker-over-graphite contamination, not a complete graphite-over-and-beside cool-gray specimen. |
