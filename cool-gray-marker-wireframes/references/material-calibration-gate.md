# Mark-making material calibration gate

This gate is the only active calibration work for this package. Desktop, mobile, annotation, and boundary generation remain suspended.

## Evidence model

Use photographs, scans, or unaltered frames from documented demonstrations of real alcohol marker on paper. Record the maker, marker family and code when available, nib, paper, capture method, creator, source URL, and reuse limits.

The primary specimen family must keep paper, scale, lighting, marker family, and crop fixed while changing one stroke variable at a time. Supporting sources may establish a separate boundary such as cool-gray layering, graphite interaction, or paper bleeding, but each must be labeled with its narrower role. Do not describe specimens from different capture sessions as matched.

## Required specimens

1. One broad chisel stroke that shows nib direction and full stroke width.
2. Two overlapping broad strokes with a clearly darker intersection.
3. One filled rectangle made from visible, parallel directional passes.
4. One curved broad stroke that shows pooling at a start, stop, turn, or edge.
5. Graphite drawn over dry marker and beside the same marker area.
6. The rejected `flat-digital-marks-01.png` comparison, visibly labeled as rejected.

The evidence set must also make these comparisons legible: one pass, two passes, blended passes, acceptable bleeding, and excessive bleeding.

## Material-first review

Review material behavior before color accuracy. Reject a specimen if its capture hides chisel direction, internal streaking, single-pass translucency, overlap buildup, localized pooling, subtle capillary feathering, pass differences, graphite interaction, or the bleeding boundary.

Reject any digital specimen that reads as a CSS fill, vector shape, airbrush shading, watercolor wash, or generic paper texture. Texture overlays, random noise, blurred fills, SVG displacement, and uniformly translucent rectangles are prohibited substitutes.

## Approval

The materials-only sheet contains no UI, cards, dashboards, devices, or overlaid callouts. Short specimen labels may sit outside the source images. The sheet remains a calibration candidate until the user explicitly approves it. Only then may the mark-making rules receive approved evidence, followed by a fresh forward test. Screen generation remains suspended until that approval.
