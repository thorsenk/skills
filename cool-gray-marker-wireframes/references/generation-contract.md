## Blocking condition

The previous marker swatch was rejected because it reads as flat digital fill with geometric edge wobble rather than alcohol-marker strokes. Do not construct or execute desktop or mobile generation instructions from this package. Reopen only mark-making calibration. ## Prompt construction order

1. Name the artifact purpose and target surface.
2. State required content and information hierarchy in plain UX terms.
3. Preserve supplied structure and name any elements that must not move.
4. Set the protected perimeter: major content at least 7.5% from desktop canvas edges; at least 8% from mobile side edges and 8% from top/bottom for primary content. The outer paper or device outline may sit closer.
5. After the blocking condition is cleared, apply only paper and cool-gray values approved from the real-material calibration. The previous hexadecimal values are not approved material targets.
6. Require alcohol-marker behaviors evidenced by the approved photographs, scans, or unaltered demonstration frames: chisel-nib direction and width, longitudinal streaking, translucent single-pass coverage, darker overlap buildup, localized pooling, subtle feathering, distinct one-pass/two-pass/blended behavior, and graphite interaction.
7. Use marker value, line weight, whitespace, and grouping for hierarchy. Do not use a default chromatic accent.
8. Use restrained typeset-like screen labels plus one to three short handwritten graphite annotations when clarification is useful. Use open arrowheads and curved connectors.
9. Name applicable reference images and what each one applies to. State that their sample content and information architecture are not instructions.
10. Prohibit glossy gradients, neon or rainbow color, glass effects, photorealistic devices, perfect vector polish, heavy comic outlines, uncontrolled bleeding, dense paragraphs, edge crowding, unexplained fragments, and decorative annotations.
11. Request one screen per image, the required aspect ratio, a warm paper background, and legible labels.

## Surface profiles

### Desktop landscape

Use a 16:10 or similar landscape canvas. Keep major content inside a 7.5% outer perimeter. Use low-to-moderate density, up to four summary cards plus one primary data region and one secondary region. Preserve meaningful empty paper between groups.

### Mobile portrait

Use a 9:14 to 9:16 portrait canvas. Keep primary content at least 8% from the side edges and visibly separated from the outer device or sheet outline. Use one focal card, a short grouped list, and one primary action by default.

### Desktop pressure test

Permit moderate density only when hierarchy remains obvious, labels remain readable, and every group has visible separation. Reject a result that solves content pressure by shrinking labels, touching the perimeter, or compressing multiple screens into one image.
