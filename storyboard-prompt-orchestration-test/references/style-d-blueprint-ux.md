## Visual goal

Create a conceptual blueprint for software behavior, UX structure, or system
architecture. It should resemble a careful technical drawing made by a product
designer: modular, cool-toned, and precise, with enough construction evidence to
remain a concept artifact rather than a finished diagram exported from software.

## Non-negotiable signature

- Use a muted deep navy-blue drafting field with pale cyan, blue-white, and soft
  gray linework. Keep the field matte and flat, never gradient-filled.
- Show a visible but quiet construction grid, alignment ticks, registration marks,
  and measured spacing guides.
- Build modular interface or service frames with a clear structural hierarchy.
- Use orthogonal connectors for primary flows, dashed paths for optional or hidden
  states, and numbered nodes for sequence or reference.
- Include schematic UI fragments, data or state labels, a compact legend, and one
  clearly emphasized critical path.
- Keep every line intentional; precision must still show slight hand-drafted variation.
- Reserve broad unmarked blue field around and between functional groups.

## Composition grammar

- Organize the schematic on an underlying grid with one primary flow direction:
  left-to-right, top-to-bottom, or center-out.
- Use three hierarchy levels: system or journey groups, component frames, and
  fine-grained states or annotations.
- Route connectors cleanly around nodes. Minimize crossings and use junction dots
  only where lines truly connect.
- Use dashed boundaries for scope, trust, optional branches, or future states.
- Keep repeated components consistent in size and spacing without becoming a
  polished auto-layout diagram.
- Keep the entire structure inside the global safe area and preserve open field
  around the legend, title, and terminal nodes.

## Mark-making and material

- Use pale technical pencil, ink, or chalk-like drafting strokes on the blue field.
- Vary line weight: heavy group boundaries, medium component frames, fine guides.
- Allow minor line overshoot, construction ticks, and hand-drafted imperfections.
- Use simple schematic symbols drawn from the same line system; no icon library.
- Avoid soft watercolor, marker shading, paper collage, drop shadows, and 3D depth.

## Color system

- Background: matte muted navy or blue-gray.
- Primary structure: pale cyan or blue-white.
- Secondary guides: desaturated steel blue or cool gray.
- Critical path: one restrained amber or coral accent.
- Exception or failure: sparse muted red, only when semantically necessary.
- Never introduce a rainbow palette, neon glow, or decorative color blocks.

## Lettering and annotations

- Use compact hand-drafted technical block lettering, precise but visibly drawn.
- Establish three text scales: section labels, node labels, and small annotations.
- Use node ids, short state names, numbered callouts, and a small legend.
- Never use UI, presentation, or generic digital fonts unless the user explicitly
  overrides the hand-drafted treatment.
- Keep labels horizontal where possible and avoid paragraph-length copy.


## Drift exclusions

- A polished flowchart, architecture deck, or auto-layout diagram export.
- Warm paper, watercolor, whiteboard marker, sticky-note, or industrial sketch texture.
- Decorative grids that do not govern alignment or hierarchy.
- Connector tangles, unexplained line crossings, or inconsistent node shapes.
- Neon cyberpunk glow, gradients, glass panels, 3D blocks, or drop shadows.
- Dense structures that consume the outer field or eliminate group separation.

## Style-specific QA gate

- Is the matte navy field and pale technical line system immediately recognizable?
- Does a real structural grid govern frames, spacing, and hierarchy?
- Are primary, optional, and critical paths visually distinct and semantically clear?
- Are connectors routed cleanly with minimal crossings?
- Are construction marks and drafted variation visible without looking sloppy?
- Are labels compact, hand-drafted, and organized into three scales?
- Does the artifact remain conceptual rather than polished presentation software?
- Is broad blue negative space preserved around groups and the outer boundary?
