## Visual goal

Create a credible industrial designer's concept sheet focused on physical form,
use, construction, and material decisions. It should combine a confident
three-quarter product sketch with smaller studies and disciplined annotations,
while keeping the marks visibly graphite-and-marker rather than digitally rendered.

## Non-negotiable signature

- Use a three-quarter perspective main view with strong silhouette and volume.
- Add two to four smaller studies such as side views, section cuts, grip or
  interaction details, an exploded component, or a use-context vignette.
- Retain perspective guides, center lines, ellipse construction, overdrawn search
  strokes, and a clear hierarchy from light construction to dark final contour.
- Use graphite plus translucent alcohol-marker tones, not photoreal materials.
- Show physical reasoning through component callouts, part breaks, movement arcs,
  dimension hints, and short material notes.
- Keep the object central to the page but avoid an isolated advertising hero shot.
- Use a soft marker grounding swatch or a few contact strokes, never a studio render shadow.

## Composition grammar

- Let the main perspective view occupy roughly half of the usable content area.
- Arrange smaller views asymmetrically around the main object and connect only
  related details with fine leader lines.
- Use a diagonal visual flow that follows the object's dominant axis or direction
  of use.
- Separate views with open paper rather than card containers or infographic panels.
- For digital components, show screens as simplified interface traces attached to
  the physical product, never as the dominant subject.
- Keep all perspective rays, callouts, and mini-views inside the global safe area.

## Mark-making and material

- Use light graphite search lines, darker contour reinforcement, and selective
  heavy line weight on the foreground silhouette.
- Use broad translucent marker strokes for planes and volume, with visible overlap
  at stroke edges.
- Suggest matte polymer, brushed metal, glass, rubber, fabric, or wood through
  distinct hatching and marker behavior rather than photoreal texture maps.
- Preserve small corrections and line overshoot. Do not vector-clean the drawing.
- Keep highlights as untouched paper or restrained white-pencil accents.

## Color system

- Primary structure: graphite, charcoal, and warm or cool gray marker.
- Secondary plane: one desaturated material tone such as slate, sand, or smoke blue.
- Functional accent: one restrained cobalt, burnt orange, or safety red.
- Use the accent only for controls, moving parts, touch points, or critical callouts.
- Avoid multicolor decoration, saturated gradients, metallic shine, and cinematic lighting.

## Lettering and annotations

- Use disciplined hand-lettered graphite or marker annotations.
- Favor compact uppercase or small block lettering with slight drawing irregularity.
- Use thin leader lines, numbered callouts, material abbreviations, and short notes.
- Never use digital or typeset fonts, UI typography, or a handwriting-style font.
- Keep annotation density reduced but precise; every note must explain form, use,
  assembly, material, or motion.

## Prompt lock

Copy this block without shortening it:

```text
STYLE LOCK — INDUSTRIAL CONCEPT SKETCH
Render a credible industrial designer's concept sheet in graphite and translucent
alcohol-marker on warm neutral paper. Center the physical product through a
confident three-quarter perspective main view, then support it with two to four
smaller orthographic, section, exploded, interaction, or use-context studies.
Retain perspective guides, center lines, ellipse construction, search strokes,
line overshoot, and a hierarchy from light construction marks to dark foreground
contours. Use broad visible marker strokes to explain planes and volume, distinct
hatching for material behavior, short physical-design callouts, and one restrained
functional accent color. Keep open paper between studies and avoid card containers.
All labels are disciplined hand-lettered graphite or marker annotations; no
digital or typeset fonts.
DO NOT use a flat infographic, polished CGI, photoreal material rendering, studio
lighting, generic UI boards, glossy advertising composition, or decorative branding.
```

## Drift exclusions

- A photoreal product render with sketch lines applied as a filter.
- One isolated hero object with no supporting studies or design reasoning.
- Flat infographic cards, generic interface boards, or UI-first storytelling.
- Perfect vector ellipses, smooth gradients, chrome reflections, or lens effects.
- Random color, decorative branding, lifestyle-ad styling, or cinematic scenery.
- Dense annotations that obscure the form or touch the canvas edge.

## Style-specific QA gate

- Is the physical object's silhouette and volume the unmistakable focal point?
- Are construction geometry, search strokes, and line-weight hierarchy visible?
- Are at least two supporting form, detail, section, or use studies present?
- Do marker strokes and hatching explain planes and materials without becoming CGI?
- Do annotations explain physical design decisions rather than generic features?
- Is the accent color restricted to functional emphasis?
- Are labels hand-lettered and technically disciplined?
- Does open paper separate the main view, supporting studies, and page boundary?
